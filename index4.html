    Florence Nightingale ( pioneer of nursing )

    Florence nightingale was born in 1820 int wealthy family in Italy , but she grow up and went to school in England . When she was 17 , she decided that she wanted to be a nurse , although her family did not that this was a suitable job for their young daughter . However , Florence work in London was so effective that the British government asked her to be in charge of the nursing of wounded soldiers abroad . after working in Europe , she returned to England and opened the nightingale school of nursing in London , the first school of this kind .

    Queen Victoria ( Empress of India )

    Queen Victoria was born in 24 may 1819 . She was the queen of the united kingdom of great Britain and Ireland from 1837 till her death for 36 year . On may 1876 , she adopted the additional title of empress of India . The Victorian age was a period of industrial , scientific , political and military progress within the kingdom . Her era was a great expansion of the British empire , she died at the age of 81.

    Amy Johnson ( the queen of the air )

    Amy Johnson was from the north of England and first interested in flying while she was working as secretary in London . In 1929 , she was awarded her pilot's licence and a year later tried to break the record for a solo flight from Britain to Australia . she became very popular with the British people who called her the queen of the air . She also flew planes for the British army during the second world war , and was killed in a plane crash in 1941.
